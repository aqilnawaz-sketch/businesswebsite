GUIDE 01

How to Get Pre-Arrest Bail in Bahawalpur

Pre-arrest bail, also called anticipatory bail, protects a person from arrest before an FIR is formally registered or acted upon. In Bahawalpur, applications are filed before the Sessions Court or, for more serious offences, before the Lahore High Court Bahawalpur Bench under Section 498 CrPC.

  • Application must state grounds why arrest is anticipated
  • Strong legal grounds significantly improve chances
  • Bail can be challenged — anticipate opposition arguments
  • Same-day filing available in urgent matters

GUIDE 02

Khula in Pakistan — Complete Procedure Guide

Khula is the right of a Muslim wife to seek dissolution of marriage through the Family Court. Unlike talaq, which is the husband's unilateral right, Khula requires a court decree. The procedure in Bahawalpur follows the West Pakistan Family Courts Act 1964.

  • File suit in Family Court of your residential area
  • Court issues notice — husband served formally
  • Reconciliation attempted by the court
  • Decree issued if reconciliation fails

GUIDE 03

How to Challenge an Illegal Mutation in Bahawalpur

Mutations (Intiqal) are entries in the Revenue Record transferring property ownership. Fraudulent or unlawful mutations are a common property dispute in Bahawalpur. They can be challenged before the Revenue Courts, Collector, and Board of Revenue — or through civil courts.

  • File written objection before Revenue Officer immediately
  • Collector appeal within 30 days of mutation order
  • Board of Revenue revision if Collector decides against you
  • Civil suit for declaration if all revenue remedies exhausted

What to Do If an FIA PECA Case is Filed Against You

A PECA 2016 case filed with the FIA Cybercrime Wing is serious and requires immediate legal action. From the moment you learn of a complaint or FIR, every step must be calculated. Mian Aqil Nawaz handles PECA cases from investigation through trial in Bahawalpur.

  • Secure all digital evidence — screenshots, messages, records
  • Pre-arrest bail application should be filed immediately

GUIDE 05

Difference Between Temporary & Permanent Injunction in Pakistan

Injunctions are court orders preventing a party from taking a specific action. In civil litigation in Bahawalpur, injunctions are among the most common and powerful remedies — whether to stop construction on disputed land, prevent sale of property, or freeze a transaction.

  • Temporary injunction — granted at initial hearing on prima facie case
  • Status quo order — maintains current position until hearing
  • Permanent injunction — final relief after full trial
  • Balance of convenience must favour applicant in all cases

GUIDE 06

When to File a Writ Petition Before LHC Bahawalpur Bench

The Lahore High Court Bahawalpur Bench exercises constitutional jurisdiction over government actions, unlawful orders, and fundamental rights violations in Bahawalpur division. A writ petition under Article 199 is the most powerful remedy against unlawful state action.

  • Mandamus — force a public authority to perform its legal duty
  • Certiorari — quash an illegal government or tribunal order
  • Habeas corpus — immediate release from unlawful detention
  • Service matters — challenge dismissal, retirement, or demotion
